# EPL Predictor — Sales & Marketing Plan

## The One Rule

**Credibility first. Always.**

Every marketing claim must be backed by real numbers. Never overstate accuracy. Show the validation tab. Show the limitations. Football fans and bettors are smart — they will test the model immediately and dismiss it if it feels like snake oil. Trust is the product.

---

## 5 Phases

### Phase 1 — Establish Credibility (Now)
**Goal:** Build a reputation for honest, data-driven predictions.
- Keep the Validation tab front and centre — it's the most important page on the site
- Share weekly fixture predictions on social media (Twitter/X, Reddit r/PremierLeague, r/soccer) before each matchday
- After matchday: post results vs predictions — celebrate accuracy, acknowledge misses
- Never delete or hide bad weeks
- Message: "We predicted X/10 correctly this week. Here's where we got it wrong."

### Phase 2 — Grow an Audience (Next 3 months)
**Goal:** Build a community of regular users.
- Content: pre-matchday prediction threads, model explainers ("why we predict Man City to win")
- Target communities: Reddit EPL subs, Twitter football analytics crowd, Discord servers
- SEO: long-tail keywords ("EPL match predictor", "Premier League predictions AI")
- Track: weekly active users, return visitors, social mentions
- Milestone: 1,000 weekly active users before monetising

### Phase 3 — Soft Monetisation (Month 4–6)
**Goal:** Test willingness to pay without alienating free users.
- Keep core features free forever
- **Premium tier ideas to test:**
  - Early access to fixture predictions (24h before public)
  - Confidence thresholds — email/push alert when model has >65% confidence
  - Historical prediction archive + accuracy stats per team
  - API access for developers (pay-per-call or monthly)
- Price anchor: £4.99/month or £39/year
- Use a waitlist to gauge demand before building

### Phase 4 — Scale & Partnerships (Month 6–12)
**Goal:** Expand reach and add revenue streams.
- Add more leagues (Championship, La Liga, Bundesliga) — reuse the same pipeline
- Affiliate partnerships with legal betting exchanges (Betfair, Smarkets) — earn referral fees without endorsing gambling
- Embed widget — let football blogs embed the predictor on their sites
- Press outreach: "AI built by one person beats professional prediction services"
- Milestone: £1,000 MRR

### Phase 5 — Enterprise & B2B (Year 2+)
**Goal:** Productise the model for business customers.
- White-label the predictor for sports media companies and apps
- Sell data feeds (confidence scores, predicted probabilities) to aggregator sites
- Sports analytics consulting — clubs and media companies need people who can build this
- Licensing the model to fantasy football platforms (FPL tools)

## Key Metrics to Track

**Accuracy**
- Live weekly accuracy (predictions made vs results) — track in a log
- Rolling 10-match accuracy vs validation benchmark (55.6%)
- Accuracy by confidence band — are >60% confidence picks actually right more often?

**Product**
- Weekly active users (WAU)
- Fixtures tab views (most engagement)
- Predict tab uses per session
- Bounce rate on Validation tab (are people reading it?)

**Growth**
- Referral sources (Reddit, Twitter, direct)
- Social media impressions per matchday post
- Email list size (if/when started)

**Revenue (Phase 3+)**
- Monthly recurring revenue (MRR)
- Conversion rate (free → paid)
- Affiliate click-through rate

---

## What NOT to Do

- Never claim to beat the bookies — we don't have that data and it invites regulatory scrutiny
- Never guarantee profits or imply this is a betting system
- Never pay for followers or fake social proof — the model's real track record is the only credible marketing asset
- Never launch premium features before hitting 1,000 weekly users — premature monetisation kills trust
